Growth creates the same problem at every scale: the business outruns its finance function. What that looks like — and what fixes it — depends on where you are.

Startups

Venture & Growth-Stage

Pre-seed through Series C

Your job is to hit the next milestone before the cash runs out. Ours is to make sure the numbers never get in the way — always fundable, never scrambling.

What growth demands at this stage
  • Runway you can trust — burn discipline, 13-week cash visibility, and scenario-tested plans for hitting milestones before the money runs out
  • A fundable story — model, metrics, and data room built before the raise, not during it
  • Unit economics that hold up — CAC payback, retention, and margin by cohort that survive investor stress-testing
  • Board & investor reporting on a cadence that builds confidence instead of questions
  • Clean foundations — cap table, 409A, 83(b) elections, R&D credits, and equity comp handled right the first time
  • Milestone-based hiring & spend plans so every dollar maps to the next round’s narrative
The Squad difference: senior CFO judgment from day one — years before you could justify the full-time hire.
Established Companies

Founder-Led & Sponsor-Backed

$1M – $100M EBITDA

You’ve built a real business. Now every decision — pricing, debt, acquisitions, exit timing — moves enterprise value. Growth here means professionalizing, not just growing.

What growth demands at this stage
  • FP&A that drives decisions — driver-based budgets, rolling forecasts, and variance discipline your operators actually use
  • A close the board and bank trust — fast, accurate monthly reporting with KPIs tied to value creation
  • Working capital & debt capacity — lender relationships, covenant reporting, and a balance sheet that funds growth without giving up equity
  • Margin & pricing improvement — finding the EBITDA hiding in your own P&L
  • M&A muscle — buy-side roll-ups, integration playbooks, and sell-side positioning when the time comes
  • Exit readiness — QoE prep, EBITDA normalization, and a diligence file that holds up under institutional scrutiny
The Squad difference: we run your finance function like the buyer is already looking — because one day, they will be.
